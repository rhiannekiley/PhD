About me

Psychology researcher
in cancer communication

I'm a PhD researcher in the Department of Psychology at the University of Sheffield, jointly funded by the University of Sheffield and Oracle Cancer Trust. My research sits at the intersection of health communication, psychology, and cancer care — examining how the language we use to describe head and neck cancers shapes how they are understood and experienced.

Before starting my PhD, I completed an MSc in Applied Psychology, with a dissertation focused on patient experiences of HPV diagnosis within the UK cervical screening programme. I also volunteer with a biotech company working on HPV self-sampling technology.

I chose head and neck cancers specifically because this field is critically understudied — particularly given the rapid rise of HPV-associated oropharyngeal cancer, which brings unique challenges around sexual health disclosure, stigma, and public awareness.

I'm committed to patient and public involvement as genuine co-production. My PPI group includes people with lived experience of HPV-related head and neck cancers, who actively shape research questions, methods, and interpretations throughout the project.

How the studies connect

Studies overview

Five studies,
four populations

A mixed-methods programme combining social media analysis, surveys, and qualitative interviews to examine HNC communication from every angle.

01
WP1 · Public
Social Media Discourse
Reflexive thematic analysis of Reddit responses to 5 celebrity HNC cases. 1,797 items collected Oct–Nov 2025.
Analysis underway
02
WP2 · Clinicians
HCP Communication Survey
Cross-sectional survey of 220+ UK secondary care clinicians across 14 role types — examining HPV communication knowledge, confidence, and barriers using a TDF-informed framework.
Data collection live
03
WP1 · Public
UK Public Knowledge Survey
Representative survey of ~1,500 UK adults via Prolific, using validated HBM-based HPV knowledge scales.
Planned
04
WP2 · Patients
Patient Experience Interviews
Qualitative interviews with ~15 HNC patients exploring how diagnosis terminology affects their cancer journey.
Planned
05
WP2 · Families
Family Member Interviews
Parallel interviews with ~15 family members on navigating HNC terminology and HPV-related stigma.
Planned
