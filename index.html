About me

Psychology researcher
in cancer communication

I'm a PhD researcher in the Department of Psychology at the University of Sheffield, jointly funded by the University of Sheffield and Oracle Cancer Trust. My research sits at the intersection of health communication, psychology, and cancer care — examining how the language we use to describe head and neck cancers shapes how they are understood and experienced.

Before starting my PhD, I completed an MSc in Applied Psychology, with a dissertation focused on patient experiences of HPV diagnosis within the UK cervical screening programme. I also volunteer with a biotech company working on HPV self-sampling technology.

I chose head and neck cancers specifically because this field is critically understudied — particularly given the rapid rise of HPV-associated oropharyngeal cancer, which brings unique challenges around sexual health disclosure, stigma, and public awareness.

I'm committed to patient and public involvement as genuine co-production. My PPI group includes people with lived experience of HPV-related head and neck cancers, who actively shape research questions, methods, and interpretations throughout the project.

Studies overview

Click any study card to expand it and read more detail. A mixed-methods programme combining social media analysis, surveys, and qualitative interviews.

01
WP1 · Public
Social Media Discourse
Reflexive thematic analysis of Reddit responses to 5 celebrity HNC cases.
Data collected December 2025. Final dataset of 1,797 items across five celebrity cases: Michael Douglas, Bruce Dickinson, Rhod Gilbert, Martina Navratilova, and Mark Steel. Analysis examines how HPV disclosure type shapes public discourse, terminology use, and stigma. Inductive approach following Braun & Clarke (2006, 2022).
Analysis underway
More ↓
02
WP2 · Clinicians
HCP Communication Survey
Cross-sectional survey of 220+ UK secondary care clinicians across 14 role types.
Examines HPV communication knowledge, confidence, and barriers using a TDF-informed framework. Builds on Dodd et al. (2017) with extended interprofessional comparisons. Includes ENTs, oral & maxillofacial surgeons, oncologists, clinical nurse specialists, SLTs, dietitians, and more. Implemented via Qualtrics with conditional survey logic.
Data collection live
More ↓
03
WP1 · Public
UK Public Knowledge Survey
Representative survey of ~1,500 UK adults via Prolific.
Uses validated HPV knowledge scales grounded in the Health Belief Model to assess public understanding of HNC terminology, risk factors, and HPV awareness. Includes demographic and socioeconomic variables. Informed by findings from Study 1. Cross-sectional design via Qualtrics.
Planned
More ↓
04
WP2 · Patients
Patient Experience Interviews
Qualitative interviews with ~15 HNC patients.
Explores how diagnosis terminology affects patients' cancer journey, help-seeking behaviour, and access to support. Sampling across cancer types and sociodemographic variables. Reflexive thematic analysis approach. Patient stakeholders involved from the outset in designing interview questions. Recruitment supported by Oracle Cancer Trust.
Planned
More ↓
05
WP2 · Families
Family Member Interviews
Parallel interviews with ~15 family members of HNC patients.
Investigates experiences navigating HNC terminology and supporting loved ones, including challenges related to HPV-related stigma. Options for 1-1, dyad, or focus group formats. Parallel data collection and analysis alongside Study 4 to allow topic guide modification. PPI involvement in question design.
Planned
